Project "Woman with a Candle" 2020

How I Created the Watercolor Illustration "Woman with a Candle"

I have always loved painting with watercolors. I love how the medium allows me to create smooth color transitions and create a sense of depth. When I decided to create an illustration for my postcardset, I immediately thought of a woman holding a candle. I thought it would be a very expressive and atmospheric image.

Sketch

The first step was to create a sketch. I used a simple pencil to create the basic outlines of the woman's figure and the candle. I also added a few details to get a sense of how the illustration would look.

Filling and Details

Once the sketch was complete, I began filling it in with color. I used various shades of blue and white to create a sense of a night sky. I also used darker shades to create depth and volume. I started with the background, gradually adding layers of color. I did this to create smooth transitions and achieve the desired effect. Once the background was filled in, I began adding details. I added details to the woman's figure, her face, hair, and clothing. I also added details to the candle, its wick, and flame. I used darker shades to add contrast and depth. I also used lighter shades to add light and highlights.

Final touches

The details were added, I completed the illustration by adding final touches. I signed the illustration with my name and date.

Scanning and Photoshop editing

Then I scanned the illustration on a scanner. This allowed me to transfer it to digital format. Importing to digital After scanning, I imported the illustration into Photoshop. This allowed me to adjust the brightness, contrast, and other image parameters. I also used Photoshop to add some additional effects to the illustration. For example, I added a bit of noise to make the image more atmospheric.

Discussion

I am very happy with the result. I believe that this illustration reflects my skills in using watercolors, as well as my ability to create expressive and atmospheric images. I hope that this illustration will be a great addition to my portfolio.
